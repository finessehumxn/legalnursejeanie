Insights from a legal nurse consultant

A clinician's eye on the evidence

I'm a registered nurse with hands-on experience across multiple specialties — including pain management, women's health, surgical, telehealth, and community mental health. That range matters: complex cases rarely stay in one specialty, and I can follow a patient's care across departments the way the chart actually unfolds, catching the handoffs, gaps, and decisions a single-specialty reviewer can miss.

Attorneys don't need me to tell them what they want to hear. They need someone who will read the entire record, say plainly what it shows, and stand behind that read whether it helps the case or complicates it.

Why "Legal Nurse Jeanie"

Clarity, on demand.

The name is a little playful, but the idea behind it is serious: when a case is buried in medical records, you need someone who can surface the answer that matters. That's the work — translating healthcare into something a legal team can act on, with the judgment of a nurse who has been at the bedside.
