FAQ

Common questions from attorneys

Straight answers about how We work, what We take on, and how to get started.

What does a legal nurse consultant do?

A legal nurse consultant is a registered nurse who applies clinical training to legal matters. We review medical records, build chronologies, analyze whether care met the standard, identify what's missing, and translate complex medicine into plain language your team and a jury can follow.

When in a case should We bring you in?

As early as possible. A review at intake tells you whether the medicine supports the case before you invest in it. That said, We can step in at any stage · discovery, deposition prep, or trial.

Do you work for plaintiff or defense firms?

Both. The analysis is the same either way: an honest, independent read of the record. We'll let you know early if there's any conflict.

Are you a testifying expert, or behind the scenes?

Either, depending on what the case needs. Most engagements are consulting work that's never disclosed. When a case calls for a named expert, We can provide declarations and testimony.

What types of cases do you take?

Medically complex litigation · personal injury, medical malpractice, nursing home and elder care, product and pharmaceutical liability, workers' compensation, wrongful death, birth injury, and toxic tort.

What does it cost?

Rates depend on the engagement level and scope, and I share them during your free initial consultation. Once we agree on scope, I confirm the rate and estimate in writing before any work begins.

How fast can you turn work around?

Tell us your filing or deposition date when we scope the work and We'll confirm whether it's achievable. If a deadline is tight, We'll say so up front rather than after.

How do you protect confidentiality?

Please don't send privileged or protected information until an engagement and conflict check are confirmed in writing. Once engaged, your case materials are handled confidentially.

Still have a question?

The first consultation is free. Ask us anything about your case and how We'd approach it.
