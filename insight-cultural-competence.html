Why cultural competence belongs in your medical case review

Perspective · Reading time ~3 min

Medicine isn't delivered in a vacuum. A patient's background, language, and the assumptions made about them can shape the care they receive · and the record that documents it. A reviewer who understands that reads a chart differently.

Bias leaves a trail

Pain that's under-treated, symptoms that are dismissed, or a patient labeled "noncompliant" can reflect communication and access barriers rather than the patient's choices. A culturally competent review reads those entries with that context instead of taking them at face value.

It resonates with the jury

Juries are diverse, and so are the patients and witnesses in these cases. An expert who can speak to cultural context · and who reflects the communities involved · connects in a way that strengthens your case and your credibility.

Care that sees the whole patient

Culturally safe, trauma-informed practice has been part of my nursing across reproductive health, telehealth, and community mental health settings. I bring that same lens to the record, surfacing context a narrow technical read can overlook.
