Telehealth and the changing standard of care

Telehealth · Reading time ~3 min

Telehealth has moved from the margins to the mainstream of care · and the law is catching up. Cases involving remote care raise standard-of-care questions that didn't exist a decade ago.

A different kind of encounter

Remote assessment has real limits: no hands-on exam, and reliance on what the patient reports and the technology captures. The standard of care accounts for those limits · and for when a patient should have been directed to in-person care.

Documentation and escalation

Telehealth records show what was assessed, what was advised, and whether escalation happened when it should have. Missing escalation is a common thread in these cases.

Practicing it, not guessing

We deliver telehealth care now, so our read on the remote standard of care is current · not an outsider's guess about an unfamiliar modality.
