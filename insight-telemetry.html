Telemetry and failure to rescue: what the monitoring record shows

Cardiac · Reading time ~3 min

"Failure to rescue" · when a patient's decline is detectable but not acted on in time · is one of the most consequential patterns in hospital litigation. Telemetry cases live or die on the monitoring record.

The data is there

Cardiac telemetry produces a continuous stream of information: rhythm strips, alarms, and vital-sign trends. The question is usually whether the warning signs were recognized and escalated.

Alarms and response

Alarm fatigue, silenced alarms, and delayed escalation are recurring themes. The record · and the device data behind it · often tells a different story than the narrative notes.

Reading the trend

From years on telemetry and medical-surgical floors, I know how a deteriorating patient looks on the monitor and in the chart, and how quickly the standard of care expects a nurse to act.
