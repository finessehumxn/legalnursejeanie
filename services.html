Example scenarios

Where we make the difference

Representative, illustrative situations · not actual case files · showing the kind of problem I solve.

The buried timeline

A delayed-diagnosis case where the answer is hidden in the order of events. We build the chronology that shows exactly when the warning signs appeared and when the response came.

The standard-of-care question

A malpractice matter that turns on whether the care was reasonable. We map the record against the accepted clinical standard and flag each point of deviation.

Records that don't add up

Charting gaps, late entries, or missing documents. We identify what's absent and what to request in discovery before it becomes a problem at deposition.

An injury case where the medical impact needs to be made real. We translate the clinical course into the lasting effects a jury can understand.

Full Service Detail

What attorneys receive, deliverable by deliverable.

Medical record organization and indexing

  • Chaotic productions of thousands of pages converted into indexed, searchable, chronological PDFs
  • Bates-aware pagination preserved so citations survive into motions and exhibits
  • Missing-record and gap identification with a request list for follow-up production

Case screening and merit evaluation

  • Early clinical read of the records against the alleged facts
  • Plain written opinion on clinical strengths, weaknesses, and open questions
  • Recommendation on whether expert spend is justified before you commit

Medical chronologies and timelines

  • Cited, dated chronology of care with page references to the source record
  • Parallel tracks for providers, medications, imaging, and key events
  • Delivered in your format: table, narrative, or demonstrative-ready

Standards of care analysis

  • What the applicable standard required at each decision point
  • Where the record meets it, where it deviates, and what the deviation changed
  • Deposition-sensitive documentation patterns flagged for examination

Deep record and EHR review

  • Audit trails, late entries, addenda, and metadata anomalies surfaced
  • Medication administration records reconciled against orders and notes
  • The details that hide between entries, stated plainly with citations

IME observation

  • A neutral registered nurse observer at independent medical examinations
  • Detailed written report of what was asked, done, measured, and omitted
  • Support and clear explanation for the examinee throughout

Expert witness location and vetting

  • Identification of qualified testifying experts matched to the case's clinical questions
  • Credential and publication vetting before introductions are made
  • Clinical briefing materials so your expert starts fast

Deposition, trial, and behind the scenes support

  • Question strategy and exhibit preparation grounded in the record
  • Clinical translation when testimony turns technical
  • Work delivered under your firm's brand when preferred
